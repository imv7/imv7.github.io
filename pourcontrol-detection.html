This one looks first: it wraps your container in infrared dots, builds a 3D model, checks the material against the liquid, fires a thermal pulse to find invisible cracks, then watches the fill level through heat itself — and slams the valve shut the moment anything is wrong. Thermal pulse lab — how it sees cracks

FIG. 4 · excitation 402 → capture 404 → analysis 406

Temperature decay after pulse

IR frames captured: 0 / 500
A heat pulse is fired at the container wall and 200–500 infrared frames record how the surface cools. A healthy wall sheds heat evenly and fast. A hidden crack or inclusion traps the pulse — its patch of surface cools slower, and the decay curves split. That temperature-rate difference is the defect detector: no X-ray, no contact, just heat and patience.
